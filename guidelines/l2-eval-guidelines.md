# Crypto Sensei L2 Evaluation Guidelines

This document is the source of truth for **L2 project evaluations** for Crypto Sensei / Sensei Project Evaluations.

L2 reviews are deeper due-diligence reports used after a project deserves more than an L1 screening. They must be scored, concise, partnership-focused, and risk-aware.

---

## 1. Core Rules

- Keep the exported Google Doc / Word version within **4 pages maximum**.
- Use the full L2 scoring structure.
- Score each main section out of 5.
- Include a total score out of 30.
- Keep each major section to roughly 3–5 bullets before the score/conclusion.
- Keep conclusions short and decision-driven.
- Do not add external reference links in the eval body except team LinkedIn links.
- Mention source names plainly, e.g. official website, CoinDesk, LinkedIn, Crunchbase, regulator registry.
- Do not mention XRP unless the project is XRP/XRPL-related or the user asks for XRP alignment.
- Final recommendation must protect Sensei’s reputation first.

## 3. Scoring Rules

- **4.5–5.0:** Excellent.
- **4.0–4.4:** Strong.
- **3.5–3.9:** Good but requires caution or campaign controls.
- **3.0–3.4:** Mixed.
- **2.0–2.9:** Weak.
- **1.0–1.9:** Very weak.
- **0–0.9:** Not viable.

---

## 4. Decision Labels

Use one final decision label:

- APPROVED
- SOFT APPROVED
- DECLINED
- HARD DECLINE

## 5. L2 Team Section Visual Format

The L2 team section must use this exact visual hierarchy:

```markdown
● CEO Borja Martel Seward:
     ○ Co-Founder & CEO and the strongest public trust anchor for Roxom.
     ○ Leads the Bitcoin-native capital markets narrative around global finance denominated in BTC.
     ○ LinkedIn: https://ar.linkedin.com/in/borja-martel
```

Rules:

- Parent/team-member bullet: use the larger filled bullet `●`.
- Second-level detail bullets: use the smaller hollow bullet `○`.
- Second-level bullets must be visually indented to the right under the parent bullet.
- In plain-text/Markdown output, put **5 leading spaces** before every `○`.
- Do not flush `○` bullets with `●` bullets.
- Each team member should have 1–2 achievement/strength bullets plus one LinkedIn bullet.
- The LinkedIn line is also a second-level `○` bullet.
- If no LinkedIn is found, write exactly: `LinkedIn not found`.

Correct:

```markdown
● CEO Borja Martel Seward:
     ○ Co-Founder & CEO and the strongest public trust anchor for Roxom.
     ○ Leads the Bitcoin-native capital markets narrative around global finance denominated in BTC.
     ○ LinkedIn: https://ar.linkedin.com/in/borja-martel
```

Incorrect:

```markdown
● CEO Borja Martel Seward:
○ Co-Founder & CEO and the strongest public trust anchor for Roxom.
○ Leads the Bitcoin-native capital markets narrative around global finance denominated in BTC.
○ LinkedIn: https://ar.linkedin.com/in/borja-martel
```

## 6. Team Link Verification

- Include LinkedIn links where found.
- Never add guessed or pattern-generated LinkedIn URLs.
- Verify the person appears on an official project/team/company source or reliable project-linked source.
- Verify the profile belongs to the correct person and ties to the project, or that official project context confirms the same person/role.
- If no LinkedIn is found, write exactly: `LinkedIn not found`.

---

## 7. Source and Link Style

- Team LinkedIn links are allowed.
- Do not add other external links inside the eval body.
- Mention source names plainly instead of linking them.
- Keep the report clean and copy-friendly.
